# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

All of the following — each arriving with a different intent:
- **Academic peers & researchers** — scanning her position, publications, and lab affiliation
- **Conference & media organizers** — assessing fit, credibility, booking potential
- **Journalists & editors** — looking for a quote, a bio, a face to put to the ideas
- **PhD applicants & students** — evaluating her as a potential supervisor or collaborator
- **General public** — curious about AI ethics, technology philosophy, what TU Delft is doing

Primary job on any visit: quickly understand who Olya Kudina is, what she stands for, and find the right thread to pull (a publication, a talk, a way to contact her).

## Product Purpose

A personal academic website for Olya Kudina — Associate Professor at TU Delft, co-director of the AI DeMoS Lab, philosopher of technology researching ethics, AI governance, and democracy. This is a full rebuild replacing the current olyakudina.com. The site is her public identity: it must hold together for a journalist skimming on mobile and an academic reading three publications deep.

## Brand Personality

Intellectual, determined, precise. The site carries the weight of serious ideas without academic dreariness. It feels like a person who thinks carefully and speaks plainly — not a CV, not a brochure.

## Anti-references

- Generic university department pages — boxy, flat, institution-first
- Overly safe minimal white portfolios with no visual commitment
- Design-studio-style sites that prioritise aesthetic over substance
- Heavy CV-dump layouts (wall of publication lists, no visual hierarchy)

## Design Principles

1. **Motion earns its weight** — animation is not decoration; it mirrors how ideas arrive: with intent, with timing, with consequence. Full-screen hero image with purposeful entrance motion sets the register immediately.
2. **Readable at every depth** — sans-serif body throughout for legibility and accessibility (WCAG AA minimum). Headings and display type may use serif for hierarchy, but the reading experience never fights the reader.
3. **Confidence without loudness** — the design makes strong choices but never shouts. Color, space, and scale do the work that copy shouldn't have to.
4. **Substance leads, design follows** — every section exists because the content demands it, not because portfolio templates include it.
5. **Presence without performance** — the site feels like Olya's space, not a stage. It's authoritative because it's specific, not because it's polished for its own sake.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

- WCAG AA minimum throughout
- Sans-serif body font for legibility (high x-height, open apertures preferred)
- All animations must have `prefers-reduced-motion` alternatives (crossfade or instant)
- Sufficient contrast for body and muted text — no light gray on near-white
- Keyboard navigation and focus states on all interactive elements
